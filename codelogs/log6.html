Findings

Throughout seeking out CSS sites, I learned some useful things, along with seeing all the different formats of these sites. All of the sites I sought out had different format, either being in article form, or getting straight to the point with a list of CSS tags, along with a description and or dedicated page. Personally, I prefer the sites that get straight to the point, like W3Schools and CSSReference.io. On these sites, you dont have to scroll through a whole article to find the meaning of one CSS property. My favorite, being one that stood out, would be the CSSReference. I like how each property has its own page if you click on it, featuring examples within it's explaination. I believe that every programming-helper page should have a layout like this, enabling students/learners to easily access specific properties, especially if they're looking into one in specific, like the 'flex' properties.
