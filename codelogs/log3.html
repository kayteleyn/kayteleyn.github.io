What media features are supported by media queries, such as 'orrientation' and 'max-width', and what do they do?

Four commonly used media queries are:

  • Max-width
  • min-width
  • orrientation
  • aspect-ratio

All of these have important roles, with max-width controlling styles when the viewport is below or at a certain width. Min-width, on the other hand, does the exact opposite. If the viewport is over or at a cetain width, it'll trigger the style. Both seem similar, however they're both used for different layouts, as max-width is used for mobile layouts, allowing you to shrink layouts or simplify navigation, while min-width is used more for desktop layouts, increasing the complexity as the screen becomes larger. Orrientation, like max-width, its used for mobile layouts, however orrientation is helpful for when the screen changes from horizontal-view to vertical-view. Orrientation specifically helps with landscape and portrait mode, allowing the text to not be all jumbled up when your phone flips the view to horizontal. Lastly, aspect-ratio identifies the size of the monitor, controlling how the scren is presented through the height and width, using ratios such as 16:9 and 4:3.
